IEEE Signal Processing Society Forum on Image and Video Signal Processing is scheduled on 27 March 2021. The highlights of the IEEE SPS forum are as follows:

● It is aimed at networking in the area of Image and Video Signal Processing to industry members.
● Bridge the gap between Industry and Educational Institution.
● It consists of presentations, lectures, and demos by industry experts.



Technical Description



For the past decade, the industry has been concentrating on the analysis and processing of image and video processing for deriving many analyses starting from crime scene to medical imaging. Digital images and videos are everywhere these days – in thousands of scientific (e.g., astronomical, bio-medical), consumer, industrial, and artistic applications. Moreover, they come in a wide range of the electromagnetic spectrum - from visible light and infrared to gamma rays. The expertise to process these image and video signals is an incredibly important skill to master for engineering/science students, software developers, and practicing scientists. In this SPS forum, how the concepts of Video and image processing can help in solving practical problems of commercial and scientific interests. Some of the important examples that will be covered by Industry experts like the removal of degradations images during acquisition (e.g., removing blur from a moving car), and the compression and transmission of images and videos, for economical storage and efficient transmission. The VR and AR concepts will also be covered in depth

Workshop on Augumented reality with MATLAB- Images, Video and 3D

Details of Workshop: In first 30 minutes, we plan to quickly walk through three demonstrations around the implementation of augmented-reality applications – first on images, second using a live-video, and third on a calibrated scene where a 3D shape is rendered. Although there may not be much time to delve into the mathematics behind this, we will attempt to describe the methods and parameters that a system designer must explore, to build reliable applications in these areas.

Post intro, we will attempt to have all the attendees run the demonstrations themselves – so that there’s a hands-on experience of how such systems work. We will provide starter code, and have you make minor edits to re-run these as exercises using your own video feeds (for caveats – we will also share pre-recorded videos so that in case of technical difficulties, you could at the very least run these on the pre-recorded videos).

Prerequisites: Prior knowledge of basic Image Processing is nice to have. Completion of Image Processing Onramp is preferred. Basic exposure to MATLAB as a programming language is also a nice to have. Completion of MATLAB Onramp is preferred.
